Migration from octopress to hugo

May 8, 2016

2 minute read

  • Twitter

  • LinkedIn

  • Google+

Introduction

I started blogging on January 1, 2014 as a hobby and to put a check on my discipline with planned vs realized. As I din’t had much exposure to blog and blog generators, I started with Google’s blogger and blogged about my achievements on a montly basis.

Once I gained some confidence on blogging and having decided to blog on a regular basis, I purchased a domain. Then I got introduced to the concept of static site generators and github-pages in the form of Octopress when my mentor had migrated to it.

Out of curiousity, I wanted to understand what octopress is and there started my journey towards static blogging.

Blogging with Octopress

Octopress is one of a very cool candidate in the static site generator space and enables us with a loads of configuration options. The content is written in Markdown and octopress converts it into an html page that can then be publised to github pages. This provisioning enables us to fully manage our content been published. It also enables us to import the blogs from blogger and wordpress and hence there were a lot of transitions towards static blogging.

Due to my lack of commitment, I had failed to utilize the openness. Also, the lack of knowledge on static blogging and git brought the procrastinator in me.

The pain points to me was the local setup of octopress. Setting up Ruby and octopress that shall be published to gh-pages for me, was not smooth. I must admit that it took enough time to make static site generators and github-pages work for me with a custom domain.

These pain points and procrasting behavior left me to look out for options in the same space and ended up with option like Hugo and Pelican.
